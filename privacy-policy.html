Dr. Naseer's ENT — Family Care Center ("we", "our", "the clinic") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect when you visit our clinic, use our website, contact us by phone or WhatsApp, or book an appointment — and how we use, share, and protect that information.

About this policy

This policy applies to information you provide when you interact with us through our website (this site), our phone line, our WhatsApp messaging, or in person at our Tolichowki clinic in Hyderabad. By using our website or our services you agree to the practices described here.

Information we collect

We collect only what is needed to provide care and respond to your queries:

  • Identification & contact details — your name, age, phone number, and email address when you book an appointment or send us a message.
  • Reason for visit — the symptom, concern, or service category you choose on the appointment form, and any notes you add.
  • Clinical information — medical history, examination findings, investigations, and treatment notes recorded during your consultation. These records are maintained by the treating doctor.
  • Communication records — calls, WhatsApp messages, and emails relating to your appointments or care.
  • Limited technical data — your browser type, device type, and IP address may be logged by the hosting provider for security and basic analytics. We do not currently use third-party advertising cookies.

How we use information

We use your information for the following purposes:

  • To schedule appointments and send confirmations or reminders.
  • To provide clinical care, including continuity across follow-up visits.
  • To respond to enquiries you send by phone, WhatsApp, or our contact form.
  • To maintain medical records as required by Indian healthcare regulations and professional standards.
  • To improve our service quality (in aggregate, non-identifiable form).

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal or health information to any third party for marketing purposes.

Sharing & disclosure

We share information only when it is necessary to provide care or comply with law:

  • With clinic staff directly involved in your treatment.
  • With external specialists or laboratories when a referral or investigation is required, and only with your consent.
  • With insurance providers if you choose to claim insurance and have provided the relevant authorisation.
  • With regulatory or judicial authorities where required by law (for example, notifiable diseases, court orders).

Third-party services

Our website embeds Google Maps so you can see our location, and links out to WhatsApp so you can message us directly. When you use these features, the relevant third-party (Google, WhatsApp/Meta) will collect data according to its own privacy policy. We recommend reviewing those policies.

We do not share your medical information with Google, Meta, or any other third party. Map embeds load on page view; WhatsApp links open in the WhatsApp client only after you click them.

Data security

We take reasonable steps to protect your information from loss, misuse, and unauthorised access. Paper records are stored in secured locations within the clinic. Digital communications are handled on encrypted channels where available. Access to clinical records is restricted to staff who need it to provide care.

No method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Data retention

Clinical records are retained for the period required by Indian medical record-keeping standards (generally a minimum of three years from the last consultation, longer for surgical and minor patients). Communication records (form submissions, WhatsApp threads) are retained for as long as needed for the original purpose and routine business operations. You may request earlier deletion subject to legal and regulatory limits.

Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Request access to a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of any information that is inaccurate or out of date.
  • Request deletion of your data, subject to legal record-retention requirements.
  • Withdraw consent to non-essential communications at any time.
  • Lodge a complaint with us first, and if not resolved, with the relevant data protection authority.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below.

Children's information

We provide care to children of all ages, and information about minor patients is collected from parents or legal guardians. We do not market our services to children and do not knowingly collect information directly from anyone under 18 outside of the context of providing care to them with guardian consent.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be highlighted on this page; we encourage you to review the policy periodically.
